  • Counts are unweighted MEPS 2022 person records at each derivation stage.
  • Medicare status is identified from the 2022 Full-Year Consolidated File before applying analytic exclusions.
  • Nonpositive full-year person weight and missing or invalid adult BMI are removed before the age restriction in this displayed flow so that the subsequent age exclusion shows the loss among Medicare records otherwise eligible for weighted BMI-based analyses.
  • Beneficiaries younger than 65 years, including disabled or ESRD Medicare beneficiaries aged 18-64 years, are removed after the weight and BMI restriction.
  • This flow does not exclude beneficiaries because of observed Medicare Part D status. Part D status is retained for the descriptive Part D versus non-Part D comparison in Table 1 and for Bridge/IRA policy eligibility in Tables 3a-3c.
  • The final cohort requires valid observed adult BMI because FDA GLP-1 weight-management eligibility depends on BMI thresholds.
  • The FDA GLP-1 weight-management proxy is BMI >=30, or BMI 27-29.9 plus at least one observable weight-related comorbidity proxy: hypertension, dyslipidemia, type 2 diabetes, obstructive sleep apnea, or cardiovascular disease.
  • CMS Medicare Bridge eligibility is not the analytic denominator. Bridge eligibility is applied later as a policy overlay among Part D beneficiaries using Bridge tier proxies and Bridge exclusion proxies.

  • Abbreviations: FPL, Federal Poverty Level; GLP-1RA, glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonist; MEPS, Medical Expenditure Panel Survey.
  • Population: Medicare beneficiaries aged 65 years or older in MEPS 2022 with valid observed adult BMI who met the FDA obesity/chronic weight-management GLP-1 eligibility proxy.
  • Columns compare beneficiaries with any observed Medicare Part D prescription drug coverage during the 2022 MEPS reference periods with Medicare beneficiaries who had no observed Part D coverage during those periods.
  • Estimates use the MEPS full-year person weight. Table 1 describes the Medicare aged >=65 FDA-eligible analytic population and is intentionally split by observed Part D status. Tables 3a-3c use the same FDA-eligible Medicare denominator regardless of Part D status.
  • The FDA eligibility proxy is BMI >=30, or BMI 27-29.9 plus at least one observable weight-related comorbidity proxy: hypertension, dyslipidemia, type 2 diabetes, obstructive sleep apnea, or cardiovascular disease.
  • Bridge tier proxies are retained as clinical context but do not define the overall analytic denominator. Bridge Tier 1 is BMI >=35. Bridge Tier 2 is BMI 30-34.9 plus heart failure, hypertension, or chronic kidney disease. Bridge Tier 3 is BMI 27-29.9 plus prediabetes, prior myocardial infarction, prior stroke, or peripheral artery disease.
  • Hypertension is defined by a MEPS survey hypertension diagnosis or hypertension CCSR condition coding. Heart failure is defined by a MEPS survey heart failure response or heart failure CCSR condition coding and represents any observable heart failure, not confirmed heart failure with preserved ejection fraction. Chronic kidney disease is based on chronic kidney disease CCSR condition coding and is not confirmed stage 3a or higher.
  • Prediabetes is based on ICD-10 R73. Prior myocardial infarction and prior stroke are based on MEPS survey diagnosis responses. Peripheral artery disease is based on ICD-10 I70, I73, or I74, or relevant circulatory CCSR condition coding, and is not confirmed symptomatic peripheral artery disease.
  • Bridge exclusion proxies are type 2 diabetes, obstructive sleep apnea, and MASH/liver disease. In the Bridge simulation, these variables route a beneficiary away from Bridge pricing and toward regular Part D coverage when Part D is observed. Type 2 diabetes is defined by a MEPS diabetes survey response, ICD-10 E11, or type 2 diabetes CCSR condition coding. The obstructive sleep apnea proxy uses ICD-10 G47 sleep-disorder coding, and the MASH/liver proxy uses ICD-10 K76 liver disease coding; neither is a clinical adjudication of moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea or noncirrhotic MASH.
  • Income categories are based on the Federal Poverty Level.
